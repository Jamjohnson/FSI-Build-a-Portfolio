About Me

Being raised by an entrepeneur as a father and a talented athletic mother, I was always taught to the importance of structure, leadership, fluid communication and integrity. Mutual respect is earned and cooperation is formed when people not only work well together, but when they can also adapt to change and use all possible resources to get tasks done.

I not only want to show my mindset, I want to become an asset to my employer while expanding my skills and becoming a talnted trustworthy software developer.

Like many of my generation, my work history has been a variety of "get-er-done" jobs. Meaning I did what I had to do to pay rent and help my family recover from a recession that crippled our financial situation. Remember I said entrepeneur father; there are ups and downs, unfortunately our down was deep. Although we were down, we were never out. Our family worked together to develop moderately profitable start-ups, which unknowingly awakened a latent talent within myself.

I loved tinkering, developing, creating and figuring complicated things out. This first led me to pursue a career in law/politics, but in a nutshell things didn't go well in that route. I was a bad student and soon lost passion for what I was doing. My true passion stemmed from art and creation. I would draw, create stories and dive so deep into gaming my friends and I would stay up for hours. To cut things short my life had many turns but working with my family I became the computer guy who created presentations, developed websites, collaborated on graphic designs, operated ecommerce sites and wore a plethora of hats when it came to "digital interactions". On top of being a huge fan of streaming media devices and working at a cell phone company I become even more interested in pursuing a career in tech.

The latest turn in my life has led me moving from my home of 25 out of 34 incredible years, Atlanta, GA to Virginia Beach, VA. The "get-er-dones" weren't getting it done and I needed a change. I hoped to get into the military contractor game and find a way into the STEM industry. Then Covid happened. To this day I believe I caught it in late fall 2019. Sickest I ever been! Covid led to a pivot, pointing me in the direction of a temp agency who got me into an IT Service Desk position.

I currently work at Hexaware Technologies, a tech company that offers a wide array of services for pharmaceutical/health sciences industry. My current role has been IT Support Analyst.
